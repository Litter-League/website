Who We Are

About the
Litter League

We're a scrappy, community-driven nonprofit on a mission to rid our streets, parks, and waterways of litter — and build a culture where people actually give a damn about their environment.

Est. 2019 · 501(c)(3) Nonprofit

Our Story

Born from a single bag and a big idea

It started in 2019, when our founder walked home through a littered park and thought: someone should do something about this. Then she realized — that someone could be her. She grabbed a bag, picked up litter for an hour, and posted about it online.

Within a week, 40 strangers showed up to help. Within a year, the Litter League was officially incorporated as a nonprofit. Today, we're a movement of thousands spanning dozens of neighborhoods, running regular cleanups, school programs, and policy campaigns.

We chose a rat as our mascot because rats get a bad rap — but they're actually incredibly resilient, community-minded, and adaptable. Just like us. Our caped rat hero represents the everyday person who decides to suit up and fight for their community.

What Guides Us

Our Core Values

💪

Community First

Every decision we make is grounded in what's best for the communities we serve — not what's easiest for us.

📢

Radical Accountability

We publish our data, finances, and outcomes openly. If we say we'll pick it up, we pick it up.

🌊

Systems Thinking

Cleanups are a bandage. We also attack the root causes of litter through education and policy advocacy.

🤝

Radical Inclusion

Anyone can join. No experience needed. No judgment. Bring yourself, we'll bring the gloves.

🎉

Joy in the Work

Fighting litter doesn't have to be grim. We bring music, snacks, and good vibes to every event.

📈

Evidence-Based Impact

We count every piece we pick up, map every hotspot, and report outcomes honestly — good and bad.
