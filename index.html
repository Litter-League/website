The Litter League is a science-based community outreach organization on a mission to end plastic pollution in built and natural environments. We launched in July 2026 and look forward to growing our membership with students at all levels, researchers, community members, staff and more. The Litter League was founded by Dr. Zoie Diana, Assistant Professor, Environmental Science Department at Barnard College, Columbia University in NYC.

Our Goals

What We're Setting Out to Do

Our goals:

🎓
Education & Awareness

Increase education and awareness of plastic pollution through workshops, education and outreach.

🧹
Litter Reduction

Reduce litter from the environment through litter cleanups and, in the future, trash trapping in local waterways.

🌿
Pollution Prevention

Work to stop new litter from entering the environment in the first place, through behaviour change and community engagement.

🔬
Science-Based Policy

Collaborate with stakeholders to inform evidence- and science-based environmental policy and management decisions.

🌍
Global Knowledge Sharing

Publish our data and lessons learned openly and widely — inspiring communities around the world to take action.

🌱
Training & Leadership

Provide hands-on training and leadership opportunities to all Litter League members.
